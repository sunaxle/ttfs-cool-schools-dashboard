Schoeneman Maintenance Model

Planting a microforest is only day one. Schoeneman (1994) warns that without forecasting structural maintenance, urban forests fail.

  • Pruning: Rapid-growth species (like Mexican Sycamore) require structural pruning every 3 years to prevent wind-shear damage.
  • Drought: During severe South Texas dry events, localized watering is required to maintain the 85% canopy target.
  • Mortality: A baseline 5-8% natural mortality rate is scientifically expected over a 10-year curve, requiring periodic "gap filling".
