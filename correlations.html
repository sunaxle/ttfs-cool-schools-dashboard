Experimental Findings & Mock Correlations

Visualizing future hypothetical datasets generated by cross-referencing spatial campus infrastructure (Tree Canopy, Trails, Soil) against student academic and behavioral metrics. These mock charts demonstrate the intended long-term analytical capacity of the platform.

EducationInfrastructure

1. Canopy Cover % vs. Mathematics Test Scores

Mock correlation exploring if spatial proximity to dense microforest trails improves quantitative cognitive focus.

Hypothesis: Positive linear correlation. Classrooms with windows facing dense canopy (>50%) show a ~12% increase in math retention compared to classrooms facing asphalt.
Education

2. Canopy Cover vs. Reading Comprehension

Exploring metrics that act independently of tree density.

Hypothesis: Independent/Plateau. Reading scores seem largely independent of exterior green views, peaking early and plateauing regardless of localized tree density.

3. Test Performance by Age Group (High Canopy exposure)

Does the presence of a microforest help all ages equally?

Hypothesis: The cognitive restoration effect of the forest (Wolf, 2010) is significantly stronger for younger grades (Pre-k through 2nd) compared to 5th grade.
Behavioral

4. Outdoor Structured Hours vs. Disciplinary Incidents

Tracking the reduction of behavioral clinic visits based on time spent on organic trails.

Hypothesis: Strong inverse correlation. For every +2 hours of structured outdoor science lab instruction per week, weekly behavioral incident reports drop by ~4.
EcologySocial

5. Campus Biodiversity vs. Student Social Interaction Rate

Measuring bird/insect species counts in a zone against the number of active student peer groups (Campbell, 2022).

Hypothesis: High biodiversity zones (microforests) attract unstructured, communal gathering, tripling social interaction rates compared to barren structural playgrounds.
HealthSensors

6. Actual Ambient Temp vs. Perceived Thermal Comfort

Comparing live air temperature sensors against the student Wellbeing Kiosk 'Sad/Red' feedback rates.

Hypothesis: Transpiration cooling tricks the body. Even on 95°F days, students in the microforest log "Pleasant" feedback, while those on 90°F asphalt log "Uncomfortable".
Health

7. Proximity to Microforest vs. Asthma Clinic Visits

Testing localized particulate filtration rates (Escobedo, 2011) against nurse visit logs.

Hypothesis: Classrooms physically located immediately downwind of the dense canopy filter report 22% fewer respiratory-related nurse visits during allergy season.
Infrastructure

8. Hardscape Removal vs. Stormwater Flooding Days

Tracking the conversion of concrete to permeable trails explicitly against parking lot flooding days.

Hypothesis: Removing just 15% of the impermeable hardscape and replacing it with root-sponge trails reduces severe pooling/flooding events by 80%.
Economics

9. Annual Structural Maintenance Budget vs. Tree Mortality

Schoeneman (1994) correlation of how investment prevents canopy collapse.

Hypothesis: Defunding maintenance below $5,000/yr creates an exponential spike in tree mortality (lost assets) the following season due to drought stress.
EcologyEconomics

10. Tree Diversity Index vs. Pest Resilience Risk

Tracking the Tree Campus USA standard ("No species > 10%") against expected financial loss from regional blights.

Hypothesis: Monocultures (single species planting) invite catastrophic 100% loss. A diversity index enforcing the <10% rule mathematically limits total disaster loss to under $20k.
